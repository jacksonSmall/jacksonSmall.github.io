## Featured Projects

### 🧠 Theoretical Analysis of CNNs for Seizure Detection
**Undergraduate Honors Thesis | Fall 2025**

Bridged deep learning application and mathematical theory to detect epileptic seizures from EEG signals. Achieved 97% accuracy while establishing Lipschitz stability bounds (K ≤ 1.5) and identifying the 22 Hz beta-wave band as the primary discriminative feature.

- **Technologies:** PyTorch (MPS), SMOTE, DFT, Butterworth Filtering
- **Dataset:** University of Bonn EEG (100 seizure + 100 healthy recordings)
- **Recognition:** Presented at Burnett Honors College Family Weekend, manuscript under review

[View Project Details](/portfolio/thesis/) | [GitHub Repository](https://github.com/jacksonSmall/seizure-detection-thesis)

### 🚗 AI Driver Risk Scoring Platform
**Independent Project | September 2025**

End-to-end telematics platform simulating driving behavior, engineering 47 risk features, and deploying a real-time Streamlit dashboard with gamification elements.

- **ML Performance:** 0.98 ROC-AUC (Random Forest, 500 trees)
- **Features:** GPS tracking, harsh braking detection, speed profiling, rewards system
- **Impact:** 30% premium differentiation, 23% reduction in harsh braking

[View Project Details](/portfolio/telematics/) | [GitHub Repository](https://github.com/jacksonSmall/Small_Jackson_TelematicsInsurance)

### 🛒 E-Commerce Shopper Behavior Analysis
**Academic Project (UCF) | Oct–Dec 2025**

Predicted purchase intent from 12,330 online shopping sessions using K-Means clustering, SMOTE, and ensemble methods.

- **Performance:** 0.93 AUC, 0.91 F1-Score
- **Methods:** 5-means clustering, ANOVA/Chi-Square testing, Random Forest (300 trees)
- **Key Insight:** PageValues accounts for 34.2% of model decisions

[View Project Details](/portfolio/shopper-behavior/) | [GitHub Repository](https://github.com/jacksonSmall/shopper-behavior-analysis)

## What Drives Me

I'm fascinated by the intersection of **mathematical theory** and **practical ML engineering**. My Honors Thesis explored Lipschitz stability bounds not just as an academic exercise, but to ensure CNN models remain robust in real-world clinical settings with noisy EEG data. Similarly, my work at Insurity isn't just about "migration"—it's about architecting scalable, compliant systems that deliver measurable ROI.

I believe the best ML engineers are those who:
- **Understand the math** behind the models (not just `model.fit()`)
- **Ship production code** that solves real business problems
- **Communicate clearly** to both technical and non-technical stakeholders
- **Iterate based on metrics** (accuracy, ROI, user engagement)

My personal connection to epilepsy research motivates my work in healthcare AI, where robust, interpretable models can literally save lives.
